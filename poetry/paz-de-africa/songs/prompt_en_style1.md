# 🇬🇧 Peace of Africa — English Version — Style 1

## Style of Music (paste in Suno)
```
deep house vocal, soulful female vocals prominent, warm bass, african percussion accents, emotional melodic, clear articulation
```

## Lyrics (paste in Suno)
```
[Intro - Deep house groove fades in with soft African djembe, 4 bars]

[Verse 1 - Voice smooth, intimate, riding the groove]
African and Africanist.
European and Spanish.
Born in Spain and Morocco.

[Verse 2 - Bass deepens, voice flows naturally]
In this lyrical poetic journey...
I return again
To this vast continent
Adjacent to Spain
Container of my cradle, Ifni.

[Verse 3 - Emotional depth, voice breaks slightly with feeling]
I was born and reborn in Sidi Ifni.
Survivor of my difficult birth,
In the second half of the twentieth century.
Three years of calamities and fears,
survivor of the Ifni war.

[Chorus 1 - Full deep house groove, voice rises joyfully above the beat]
Then came ten happy years,
With my parents,
my two little sisters.
I had five unborn siblings.
So many friends, so much love.
Parties, music and dances.

[Verse 4 - Playful, light percussion, voice smiling]
Learning and reading;
drawing and painting.
Bicycle, skating and swimming.
Singing and playing the guitar.

[Verse 5 - Warm, spiritual, synth pads bloom]
My vital spirit was reborn,
To Peace and Art,
In my beloved Sidi Ifni.
On top of the cliff
with its fringed horizon.

[Chorus 2 - Peak energy, voice powerful and clear]
Of bluish cobalt color.
Where the setting sun
bids farewell to the bathed day.
In the sea of the seven waves.
A small and big treasure.
Paradise of my childhood.
Always there my heart!

[Bridge - Beat strips back, just bass and voice, raw emotion]
Who would have told me that…
Ifni would abandon me at fourteen?
Exiled from my beautiful Africa.
To avoid another war.
In Spain I lived without my ocean.

[Verse 6 - Tender return, soft piano over groove]
After twenty-five years
I returned to visit…
My house without garden, nor orchard.
Intact the love of the people.
Their friendly hospitality.

[Climax - Full crescendo, voice triumphant]
Now I think about returning…
To breathe my ocean.
And live the Peace that I was there.

[Outro - Beat fades, voice a cappella with reverb]
Thank you, María Paz Arés Osset,
for this poem where you bare your soul.
Your love for Africa lives in every line,
in every wave that kisses your memory.
Sidi Ifni lives in you,
and you live in Sidi Ifni.
Peace… always Peace.

[Fade out with ocean soundscape and distant djembe]
```
