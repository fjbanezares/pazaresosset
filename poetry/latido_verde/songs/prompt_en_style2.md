# 🇬🇧 Green Heartbeat — English Version — Style 2 (Spoken Word × UK Deep House)
**Genre:** Spoken Word Poetry Battle × UK Deep House
**Vibe:** A London rooftop at sunset. UK spoken word tradition meets deep house — that uniquely British combination of poetic flow and bass-driven warmth. He delivers the protest verses with the measured cadence of a slam poet; she responds with soaring melodies that float above the beat. The production is slick and warm: deep sub-bass, garage-inflected shuffled hi-hats, warm chords, and a vocal-driven mix that makes every word land. Think James Blake's intimacy meets Rudimental's uplift. The English version should feel URBAN and POETIC, not folky.

[Intro - 8 bars, deep sub-bass, warm chords, shuffled hi-hat, city atmosphere SFX]
(NO vocals — urban sunset rooftop atmosphere, warm bass pulse)

(Beautiful female vocal, clear, soaring, DOMINANT in the mix)
Through forests and gardens,
my spirit contemplates... and feels.
I embrace each tree,
sensing its la-tent... bea-ting.

In every leaf that falls,
in every branch that sways — you hear it?

that makes my soul... tremble.

[Build - Bass deepens, synth pads swell, tension rising]

[Verse 2 - FEMALE, gaining energy, like a rallying cry]

With hope I join the poets,
souls who rhyme with love.
Together we raise our voice...

[Verse 2b - MALE, completing her thought, rhythmic spoken word]

...in a pro-tec-tive echo.
We sing about fruit trees,
of dazzling colors bright,
about birds that fly above us
with songs that ne-ver fade from sight.

[Drop 1 - BOTH TOGETHER, euphoric synth lift, bass drops, HANDS IN THE AIR]

Join this song right now,
let it resound in every soul!

[Verse 3 - MALE solo, spoken word protest, intensity rises]

Because felling without reason,
just to build without end,

is to stop... brea-thing.

[Break - 4 bars, just piano and bass, breathing space]

The trees give us life,

With poems and art,
I want to teach everyone:
the urgency of saving trees...

[Verse 4b - MALE whispers, completing]

[Chorus - BOTH, maximum beauty, synths wide open, euphoric]

Let us love the trees,
let us tend them with devotion...

we find our sal-va-tion.

[Verse 5 - ALTERNATING line by line, intimate call and response]

[FEMALE - closing with gentle strength]

[Outro - Beat dissolves, FEMALE voice alone, almost spoken]

After writing the poem,
I go to embrace all the trees...
from now on, to greet them,
and exchange energy.

[Spoken - MALE, warm deep voice over last reverb tail]
Thank you, Paz Arés Osset,
for reminding us that a tree
has been breathing for us
long before we knew its name.

The second best time... is now."
